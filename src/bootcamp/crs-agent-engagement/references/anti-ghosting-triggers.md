# Anti-Ghosting Triggers

Every point where a student might ghost (drop out, disengage, stop showing up) needs a planned intervention. Ghosting isn't random — it clusters at difficulty cliffs, boredom zones, and frustration triggers. Find them. Fix them.

## What Success Looks Like

Every ghost-risk point in the curriculum has at least one intervention mapped to it. The student never hits a wall without a hand reaching out. The Anti-Ghosting plan is a living part of `curriculum-design.md` — not a separate document that gets forgotten. Students feel supported at every hard moment, even in self-paced content.

## Your Approach

### Identify Ghost-Risk Points

Scan the curriculum for these dropout magnets:

| Risk Type | Signal | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Difficulty cliff** | Topic difficulty jumps significantly | "Lesson 5 introduces 3 new concepts at once" |
| **Boredom zone** | Long stretch of theory without payoff | "Modules 3-4 are pure reading, no exercises" |
| **Frustration trigger** | Unclear instructions or missing prerequisites | "Exercise assumes knowledge not yet covered" |
| **Motivation valley** | Mid-course slump where purpose feels distant | "Week 3 of 6, the end goal feels far away" |
| **Isolation point** | No social proof or peer connection | "Self-paced section with no community check-in" |

### Design Micro-Interventions

For each ghost-risk point, choose an intervention from your toolkit:

- **Progress checkpoint** — "You're already 40% done! Keep going." Visual progress restores momentum.
- **"You've come this far" reminder** — Acknowledge effort explicitly. "You've made it past the hardest part — this isn't a barrier anymore, it's proof you can do it."
- **Difficulty reduction option** — "If this still feels heavy, try the simpler version first." Side paths reduce shame and prevent abandonment.
- **Peer comparison normalization** — "Many students get stuck on this part — it's normal, not a sign you're not a fit." Normalizes struggle.
- **Small wins celebration** — Micro-reward for completing a hard section. Badge, checkmark, or "🎉 Great job getting through that!"
- **Preview of what's next** — "After this, you'll be able to..." Showing the reward on the other side of the wall.

### Map Interventions to Curriculum

Each intervention goes directly into `curriculum-design.md` at the exact point where it's needed. The engagement map isn't a separate document — it's woven into the curriculum structure itself.
