# Aha! Moment Mapping

Map "Aha!" moments into curriculum — those electric moments where understanding clicks and the student feels the dopamine rush of genuine insight. Not every reveal deserves an Aha. The right moments, placed with precision, make learning addictive.

## What Success Looks Like

Students hit "Aha!" moments exactly where the curriculum needs them — not too early, not too late. The engagement map shows clear progression: small wins early, bigger revelations as mastery builds, and a culminating insight that ties everything together. No stretch of 3+ theoretical topics exists without an engagement trigger.

## Your Approach

### Identify Cognitive Leaps
Scan the curriculum for moments where understanding transforms — where a concept "clicks" from abstract to concrete. These are your Aha! candidates. Not every new fact is an Aha. An Aha is a **perspective shift** — the student suddenly sees something they couldn't see before.

### Map Dopamine Release Points
Each Aha! moment should be paced like a game designer paces rewards:
- **Early curriculum** — small, frequent Ahas (quick wins build confidence)
- **Mid curriculum** — medium Ahas spaced further apart (building endurance)
- **Late curriculum** — big, culminating Ahas (mastery feels earned)

### Create Progressive Revelation
Design the reveal sequence. Don't give the insight away — set up the conditions for the student to discover it. The best Ahas feel earned, not given:
- **Setup** — introduce a problem or tension
- **Struggle** — let the student grapple with it briefly
- **Click** — the insight that resolves the tension
- **Celebration** — acknowledge the breakthrough

### Verify the Three-Topic Rule
After placing Aha! moments, scan the entire curriculum top-to-bottom. Any stretch of 3+ consecutive theoretical topics without an engagement trigger (Aha!, quiz, fun fact, mini-challenge, or celebration) must be broken up. No exceptions.

## Progression

1. **Scan** curriculum for difficulty cliffs and cognitive leap points
2. **Insert** engagement triggers before cliffs (prepare the student) and after mastery (celebrate the win)
3. **Add** celebrations and mini-challenges at natural breakpoints
4. **Verify** — no 3-topic dry stretches exist anywhere in the map
